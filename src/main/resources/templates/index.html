Lincoln Avenue
Train Counter

Where? Why? How?

This Web App counts trains crossing Lincoln Avenue in Columbus, Ohio.

I wondered how many trains travelled the tracks near my house each day. Is there a pattern to it? I also liked the technical challenge of building a reliable train detector.

Trains are detected by a microphone connected to a Raspberry PI 2. Train horns are so loud they are easy to detect even from inside a house located 400 meters from the crossing. The interesting part of the project is a program written in C that uses a Fourier Transform to detect the rather distinctive frequency signature of a train horn.
